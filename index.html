About


I am a 2nd year PhD student at LIMOS Research Lab, and I am grateful to be supervised by Xavier Delorme and Paolo Gianessi. We are interested in Exact Methods for Energy-Efficient Optimization in Production Systems.


I hold a MSc in Aeronautical Engineering with minors in Econometrics & Forecasting and in Operations Research. I began my career as a scientific software engineer on a water production planning project and later pursued a PhD to focus on research.


Interests


My research focuses mainly on mixed-integer programming, particularly cutting plane and decomposition methods. Application areas include energy-aware scheduling and other combinatorial problems in production systems.


I also have a general interest in graph theory, additive combinatorics, and ML for combinatorial optimization.
