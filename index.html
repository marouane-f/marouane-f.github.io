About


I am a 2nd year PhD student at LIMOS Research Lab, and I am grateful to be supervised by Xavier Delorme and Paolo Gianessi. We are interested in Exact Methods for Energy-Efficient Optimization in Production Systems.


I hold a MSc in Aeronautical Engineering with minors in Econometrics & Forecasting and in Operations Research. Following graduation, I chose to focus entirely on OR and started my career as a scientific software engineer on a water production planning project. Roughly a year later, I got a heart calling for scientific research and quit my job to pursue a PhD.


Interests


My research focuses mainly on mixed-integer programming with interests in

  • cutting planes and decomposition methods
  • ML for combinatorial optimization

and applications to energy-aware scheduling/other problems in production systems. Energy can be taken into account through different cost functions
  • any linear, particularly piece-wise constant functions (e.g. electricity tariffs)
  • any non-linear function (e.g. a non-reversible energy source)
    • and different constraints/considerations
    • a capacity/knapsack constraint (e.g. max. power peak at all times)
    • setup/idle power usage

      • In my spare time, I enjoy football, hiking, and volunteering.
