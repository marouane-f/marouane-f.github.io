About


I am a 1st year PhD student at LIMOS Research Lab, and I am grateful to be supervised by Xavier Delorme and Paolo Gianessi. We are interested in Exact Methods for Energy-Efficient Optimization in Production Systems.


I hold a MSc in Aeronautical Engineering with minors in Statistics, Econometrics & Forecasting and in Operations Research. Following graduation, I chose to focus entirely on OR and started my career as a scientific software engineer on a water production planning project. Roughly a year later, I got a heart calling for scientific research and quit my job to pursue a PhD.


Interests


My research interests mainly revolve around

  • mixed-integer-programming
  • polyhedral approaches
  • cut/column generation
  • ML for discrete optimization

with applications to (under energy consideration)
  • shop and machine scheduling
  • line balancing
  • production planning

In my spare time, I enjoy football, hiking, and helping people through volunteering and online communities.
